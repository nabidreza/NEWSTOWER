Freedom fighter killed over land dispute in Cumilla

Police today recovered the body of a freedom fighter, who was killed during a quarrel with neighbours, in Cumilla's Brahmanpara upazila today.

The deceased was identified as Abdur Rahman (70), of Kandughur village of the upazila, reports our local correspondent quoting Nazmul Huda, inspector (investigation) of Brahmanpara Police Station.

Quoting locals, Nazmul said the freedom fighter was strangled to death allegedly by his neighbours over land related dispute.

The police official said an altercation broke out among the neighbours -- Kamal Hossain and his brother Md Babu -- when Abdur Rahman's younger brother Karim Miah and his sons went to repair the aisle of a land, situated on the south side of their house.

At one stage, Kamal and his brother started beating Karim Miah and his sons with strict, the inspector said, adding that later freedom fighter Abdur Rahman came forward to save his brother and was beaten.

"When Abdur Rahman fell down on the ground, Kamal and his brothers strangled him (Abdur Rahman) -- leading to his death on the spot," the inspector said quoting locals.

The accused Kamal Hossain and his brothers remained absconding following the incident, the police officer informed.

The victim was a well-known freedom fighter in the locality, said district Muktijuddha Sangsad

Opella Raju Naha, officer-in-charge of Brahmanpara Police Station, said the body was sent for autopsy

The matter is being investigated, the OC said, adding that action will be taken against the accused in the incident.
